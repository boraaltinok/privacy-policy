## 2. Microphone and Audio Data

The Application uses your device's microphone so you can record your cat's meow and receive a playful, for-entertainment interpretation.

- **Raw audio recordings are never uploaded and are never stored on our servers.** A short recording is written to a temporary location on your device only, used to compute a few derived numeric loudness characteristics, and then discarded by the operating system.
- The only audio-related data that may leave your device are **derived numeric features** — for example, the meow's duration in milliseconds, an average loudness value, and a near-clipping ratio. These contain no audio content and cannot be used to reconstruct what was recorded.
- These derived features, together with the context tag and cat nickname you entered (see Section 1.3), may be sent to our backend interpretation service (Firebase Cloud Functions) **only when that service is available and your device is online**. When the service is unavailable or you are offline, the interpretation is generated **entirely on your device** from a bundled set of curated lines, and nothing related to your meow leaves the device.
- The "Talk to your cat" feature plays back **bundled, pre-recorded cat sounds** and does not require recording or transmitting audio.
