## Getting Started

### How do I use the calculator?
1. Open the Application — the Calculator tab is the home screen
2. Tap the number, feet, inches, and fraction keys to enter a measurement
3. Use +, −, ×, and ÷ to combine measurements, then tap **=**
4. The result is shown in feet-inch-fraction and in a decimal/metric readout at the same time

The core dimensional calculator and the unit converter are **free, unlimited, and offline forever**.

### Can I use the Application without creating an account?
Yes. The Application does not ask you to sign up, register, choose a username, log in, or provide an email address. There is no account and nothing to remember — you can start calculating the moment you open it.

### Does it work without internet?
Yes. The Application is designed to work fully offline. The calculator, all tools, the to-scale diagrams, your saved tape, and PDF export all work with no connection. A connection is only used for optional analytics, crash reporting, and making or restoring purchases.

### How do I switch between feet-inches and metric?
Use the unit toggle on the calculator keypad, or open **Settings** and change the Units option. You can also set your default fraction resolution (for example 1/16) and your theme in Settings.

## Measurement & Accuracy

### How accurate are the results?
The Application uses an exact-arithmetic engine for dimensional math (feet, inches, and fractions), so add/subtract/multiply/divide results are exact rather than rounded floating-point approximations. Trade tools use published reference constants and formulas.

That said, all results are **estimates and calculation aids**. Always verify against your on-site measurements, your local building codes, manufacturer specifications, and professional judgment before cutting material or ordering quantities. Please review the Measurement and Calculation Accuracy Disclaimer in the [Terms & Conditions](https://github.com/boraaltinok/privacy-policy/blob/main/construction-calculator-terms-conditions.md).

### What does the stair "Meets IRC code" check mean?
Some tools (such as Stairs) show a pass/fail verdict based on specific published code criteria (for example, IRC stair riser and tread limits). This is a convenience check against built-in reference values for one code standard — it is **not** a substitute for your local building code, permitting, or an inspector's review. Codes vary by jurisdiction and change over time.

### My result looks wrong
Double-check the values you entered, the active unit system (imperial vs metric), and your fraction-resolution setting. Remember that squaring/area results cannot be fed back into a linear operation — the Application will flag an invalid entry rather than produce a misleading answer. If you still believe a result is incorrect, please contact us at support@nextline.software with the exact inputs so we can look into it.

## Troubleshooting

### I purchased Pro but the tools are still locked
1. Make sure you are connected to the internet so the purchase can be verified
2. Open the paywall or **Settings** and tap **Restore**
3. Force-quit the Application and reopen it
4. Confirm you are signed in with the same Apple ID used for the purchase
5. If it still does not unlock after a minute, contact support@nextline.software

### The paywall shows "products unavailable" or a Retry button
This usually means the store could not load the current prices (for example, a temporary network issue). Check your connection and tap **Retry**. By design, the Application never shows a guessed or fallback price — it waits until the real store prices load.

### My saved tape / history disappeared
Your tape, projects, and settings are stored **locally on your device**. They are not backed up to a cloud by the Application, so uninstalling and reinstalling the Application, or moving to a new device, will not carry them over. Keep this in mind before uninstalling.

### How do I clear my saved calculations?
Swipe a row in the Tape to delete it, or long-press a project to rename or delete it. To clear everything, uninstall the Application — since all your data is stored only on your device, uninstalling removes it completely.
