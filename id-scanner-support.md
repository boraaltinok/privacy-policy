# ID Scanner: Age Check - Support

Welcome to the ID Scanner: Age Check support page. We're here to help you maximize your experience with our application.

## Getting Started

ID Scanner: Age Check helps venue staff check US and Canadian IDs by scanning the PDF417 barcode on the back of the document and returning a clear Pass / Review / Deny result — entirely on your device, even with no internet connection.

### Key Features
- **Barcode Age Check**: Scan the PDF417 barcode on the back of US and Canadian IDs for an instant Pass / Review / Deny verdict
- **Custom Age Threshold**: Set the minimum age your venue enforces (e.g., 18, 19, 21)
- **Scan History & Tonight's Stats**: Review past scans and see visitor counts and verdict breakdowns for your venue night
- **Ban & VIP Lists**: Flag documents so repeat scans are recognized instantly — without storing the actual document number
- **Log Export**: Export your scan log as CSV or PDF as proof you checked

## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: How do I get started with ID Scanner: Age Check?**
A: Download the app from the App Store, complete the short setup (choose your venue's age threshold), grant camera permission, and point the camera at the barcode on the back of an ID.

**Q: Which IDs does the app support?**
A: The app reads the PDF417 barcode found on the back of driver's licenses and ID cards issued in the United States and Canada.

**Q: Does the app work offline?**
A: Yes. Scanning, verdicts, history, and stats all work fully offline. An internet connection is only needed to purchase or restore your subscription.

**Q: Is patron data secure?**
A: Yes. All scanning happens on your device — nothing from a scanned ID is ever uploaded. Scan history is stored in an AES-encrypted local database, patron names and addresses are never stored, and document numbers are kept only as irreversible salted hashes. For more information, refer to our [Privacy Policy](https://github.com/boraaltinok/privacy-policy/blob/main/id-scanner-privacy-policy.md).

### Camera & Scanning

**Q: Why does the app need camera access?**
A: The camera is used only to read the barcode on the back of an ID. No photos are taken, stored, or uploaded. If you denied permission, enable it in Settings > ID Scanner > Camera.

**Q: The barcode won't scan. What should I do?**
A: Try these steps:
1. Make sure you are scanning the **back** of the ID (the 2D barcode area)
2. Hold the ID flat and fill the frame guide with the barcode
3. Avoid glare — tilt the card slightly away from overhead lights
4. Wipe the barcode area if it is dirty or scratched
5. If the barcode is damaged and cannot be read, check the ID manually

**Q: What does a "Review" result mean?**
A: Review means the app could not fully confirm the check — for example the barcode was unreadable, the document is expired, or data was inconsistent. The app never guesses: when in doubt it asks you to inspect the ID manually.

**Q: Does a "Pass" mean the ID is genuine?**
A: No. The app reads the data encoded in the barcode; it cannot detect forged or cloned barcodes. Always compare the photo, inspect the physical document, and follow your venue's procedures. The final decision is always yours.

## Data Management

**Q: How do I delete scan history?**
A: You can clear your scan history and counters from the app's Settings at any time. You can also remove all app data by uninstalling the Application. Because everything is stored locally, we have no copy of your data. Note: some jurisdictions limit how long venues may retain data from scanned IDs — clear your history in line with your local rules.

**Q: How do I export my scan log?**
A: Subscribers can export the scan log as a CSV or PDF from the History screen and share it via the native share sheet. Exports never include document hashes; once shared, handle exported files according to your venue's data policies.

**Q: What is stored when I ban or VIP a document?**
A: Only an irreversible, salted cryptographic hash of the document number plus your note/tag. The actual document number is never saved, so the list is meaningless outside your device.
