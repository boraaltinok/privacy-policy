# Light Meter - Support

Welcome to the Light Meter support page. We're here to help you maximize your experience with our application.

## Getting Started

Light Meter allows you to measure ambient light levels in lux and foot-candles using your device's front-facing camera sensor. The app is compatible with iOS and Android devices.

### Key Features
- **Light Level Measurement**: Measure ambient brightness in lux or foot-candles
- **Contextual Interpretations**: See what your light level means for plants, desk work, and general use
- **Two Measurement Modes**: Quick Check (instant readings) and White Paper (improved accuracy with a white sheet over the camera)
- **Spot Comparison**: Compare brightness between two locations side by side
- **Measurement History**: Save and review up to 500 readings and 500 comparisons
- **Share Cards**: Generate and share visual measurement result cards

### Camera & Permissions

**Q: Why does Light Meter need camera access?**
A: The front-facing camera sensor is used to measure ambient light levels. No photographs are taken or stored — only numerical brightness data is extracted from the camera sensor.

**Q: The app shows 0 lux or no readings. What should I do?**
A: Make sure you have granted camera permission in your device settings. Go to Settings > Light Meter > Camera and ensure it is enabled. Also verify that no other app is currently using the camera.

**Q: How accurate are the measurements?**
A: Measurements are approximate and depend on your device's camera hardware and environmental conditions. For improved accuracy, try the White Paper mode by placing a white sheet of paper over the front camera. The app is not a substitute for professional lux meters.

**Q: What is the difference between Quick Check and White Paper modes?**
A: Quick Check provides instant approximate readings without any setup. White Paper mode, where you place a white sheet of paper over the front camera, improves accuracy by providing a consistent diffused light reference for the sensor.
