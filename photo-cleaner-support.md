## Frequently Asked Questions

### General Questions

**Q: How do I get started with Photo Cleaner?**
A: Download the app, complete the short onboarding, grant photo library access when prompted, and the app will scan your library automatically.

**Q: Is my data secure?**
A: Yes. Photo Cleaner is a local-first application — all photo analysis happens entirely on your device. Your photos are never uploaded, transmitted, or accessed by us or any third party. Analytics and crash data sent to third-party services are transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS). For more information, refer to our [Privacy Policy](https://github.com/boraaltinok/privacy-policy/blob/main/photo-cleaner-privacy-policy.md).

**Q: Does the app work offline?**
A: Yes. All scanning, analysis, and cleanup features work fully offline. Only subscription management, analytics, and crash reporting require an internet connection.

### Photo Library & Permissions

**Q: Why does Photo Cleaner need photo library access?**
A: The app needs full photo library access to scan your photos and videos for duplicates, similar shots, screenshots, and large files. Your photos never leave your device — all analysis is performed locally.

**Q: I denied photo library access. How do I fix it?**
A: Go to your device Settings > Photo Cleaner > Photos and select "Full Access". The app requires full access to scan and manage your library.

**Q: Will the app delete photos without my permission?**
A: No. You always review items before deletion. When you confirm deletion, photos are moved to your device's "Recently Deleted" album, where you can recover them for up to 30 days.

### Scanning & Results

**Q: How long does scanning take?**
A: The initial scan typically takes a few seconds for metadata (screenshots, large videos) and up to 30 seconds for full analysis (duplicates, similar photos) depending on your library size. Results appear progressively as they're found.

**Q: The app didn't find all my duplicates. Why?**
A: Duplicate detection uses strict fingerprinting to avoid false positives. Photos that are visually similar but have different resolutions, crops, or edits may be grouped as "similar" rather than "exact duplicates." This is by design to protect your photos.

**Q: Can I rescan my library?**
A: Yes. Return to the results screen and use the rescan option to refresh your analysis with any new photos added since the last scan.
